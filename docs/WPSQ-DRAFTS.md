# WPSQ draft templates (reusable)

**Re:** Four scored themes common on Welsh and UK framework entries. Exact WPSQ wording sits behind the portal; treat these as skeletons to expand.

**Context:** [NRW-FRAMEWORK-STRATEGY.md](./NRW-FRAMEWORK-STRATEGY.md)

## Three rules (non-negotiable)

1. **Only claim what is real.** If the tool is at prototype or MVP stage, say you have developed an instrument and a method. Do not say you have deployed it across engagements unless you have.
2. **Lead with delivered outcomes, reference the tool as evidence of method.** Outcomes win points, tooling supports them.
3. **Bracketed placeholders mean insert a real fact or delete the sentence.** Never leave a claim you cannot evidence.

## Q1. Relevant experience

The tool should barely feature here. Lead with real engagements.

> We have delivered DDaT services for regulated and public sector clients including [name 2 to 3 genuinely comparable engagements, for example NRW, Thames Water, a central government body], spanning [service design, agile delivery, capability uplift]. On [named engagement] we [delivered a specific outcome with a metric or assessment result]. Our method operationalises the GDS Service Standard and, for Welsh services, the Digital Service Standard for Wales, including bilingual design and the wellbeing duty from the outset. [If true: We have begun applying a structured readiness and team-fit instrument we developed to make this method consistent and evidenced across engagements.]

**Caution:** Include the final sentence only if you have genuinely used the tool on a real engagement. If it is still a prototype, leave it out entirely.

## Q2. Technical and professional ability

Strongest honest home for the tool.

> We ensure technical and professional ability through a method that maps every role to the Government Digital and Data Capability Framework and assesses team readiness against the applicable service standard before and during delivery. We have operationalised this in a structured instrument that, for a given service and phase, identifies the roles and skills required, scores the proposed team against them with fully explainable reasoning, names evidence gaps against each point of the standard, and tracks agile delivery rigour beyond a binary checklist. For Welsh services this includes Welsh language service capability and the future generations wellbeing duty as first-class requirements. The instrument is advisory and supports professional judgement: it makes no automated personnel decision and uses no protected characteristics. [If true: It has been applied on [engagement] to [outcome].]

## Q3. Capacity and resources

Tie to specialist skills shortages (NRW named risk).

> We mobilise the right team for each call-off by shaping it to the specific service, phase and constraints rather than fielding a generic team. Our readiness method surfaces capability gaps, single points of failure and over-provision early, and produces an adaptation plan that flexes the team to the available budget and timeline. This directly mitigates the risk of specialist skills shortages: where a gap cannot be filled internally, we identify it up front and plan the upskilling, blending or partnering route, with indicative cost and time to competence. We sustain capability over the framework term through [stable team approach / capability transfer], reducing dependency on any single individual.

---

## Q4. Sustainability and innovation

Strong home; distinctive for Welsh clients.

> Our innovation is to operationalise public sector service and capability standards into a working instrument, rather than treating them as documents. For a Welsh service this includes bilingual capability and the wellbeing duty by design, not as an afterthought. Sustainability runs through our approach in two ways. First, we build client capability rather than dependency: our method and instrument are designed to be owned and run by the client, transferring assurance capability into the organisation so it persists beyond any single engagement. Second, [environmental or whole-life sustainability point relevant to the named services]. We continuously improve the method as the underlying frameworks evolve, so the client's assurance stays current as standards change.

## Social value (often scored separately, e.g. 10 per cent)

> We commit to building lasting digital and data capability within NRW and the wider Welsh public sector. This includes [transferring our readiness and assurance method into NRW so its own teams can specify and assure services], [supporting Welsh language digital skills], and [knowledge sharing, coaching, or local skills commitments]. Our approach is designed so that capability remains with the client after our involvement ends, supporting a more resilient and self-sufficient Welsh public sector.

Make every commitment specific and measurable. Vague social value scores poorly.
